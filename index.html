A (very brief and incomplete) History

1969
All 4 members meet for the 1st time
1970
They perform their first song "Hej, Gamle Man (Hey, Old Man)" as the group Agnetha, Anni-Frid, Benny and Björn
1972
Their first hit as Agnetha, Anni-Frid, Benny and Björn "People Need Love" is released
1973
The band's new name, ABBA, was first used
Their debut Album, Ring, Ring was released
1974
Won the 1974 Eurovision Contest with the song "Waterloo"
Their first European Tour
1975
Their single "Fernando" was released. It stayed on the Austrailian charts for an amazing 40 weeks, tying a record by The Beatles
1976
"Dancing Queen" became the group's first #1 hit song in the US
"Knowing Me, Knowing You" was their 16th song to reach #1 in Germany
1977
Their first major World Tour
ABBA: The Album was released
1978
Along with Stig Anderson, their producer, they created the Polar Music studio in Stockholm, Sweden. Albums such as Genesis' Duke and Led Zeppelin's In The Out Door were recorded there.
1979
They performed the song "Chiquitita" for United Nations' General Assembly at the Music for Unicef Conert on 9 January. This song reached Number 1 in 10 countries.
ABBA: The Tour sold out 40 concerts worldwide:
  • 13 in the USA
  • 4 in Canada
  • 23 in Western Europe, including 6 at London's Wembley Arena
1980
Their Japan Tour
Super Trouper, their 7th studio album, was released
1981
They appeared on Dick Cavett Meets ABBA, an internationally produced television special
The Visitors, their 8th and final official studio album was released
1983
Andersson and Ulvaeus work with Tim Rice on the Broadway Hit Chess
1986
Their last appearance together as ABBA
2004
The London premiere of Mamma Mia! The Musical
2008
The Swedish Premiere of Mamma Mia! (The Movie) brings all 4 members of the band together publically for the first time in over 20 years
2016
A "Reunion Project" was annoounced for 2017...
